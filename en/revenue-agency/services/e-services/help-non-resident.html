Open a non-resident tax account

When is a non-resident tax account needed?

If you or your client is a non-resident who is paid or credited certain income from Canadian sources, you need a non-resident tax account.

What is a non-resident tax account?

You use a non-resident tax account to remit Part XIII withholding tax into.

What is Part XIII tax?

Part XIII tax is the tax due on certain passive Canadian-source income that is paid or credited to any non-resident of Canada. Section 212 of the Income Tax Act, Part XIII – Tax on Income from Canada of Non-resident Persons, describes all the taxable income involved.

The most common types of Canadian income subject to Part XIII tax are:

  • dividends
  • rental and royalty payments
  • pension payments
  • old age security pension
  • Canada Pension Plan and Quebec Pension Plan benefits
  • retiring allowances
  • registered retirement savings plan payments
  • registered retirement income fund payments
  • annuity payments
  • management fees

When is Part XIII tax due?

Part XIII tax is due by the 15th of the month following the month in which the Canadian-source income was paid or credited to the non-resident.

What is a non-resident tax account owner responsible for?

The non-resident tax account owner is responsible for the following:

  • deducting non-resident Part XIII withholding tax at source
  • remitting these deductions
  • filing the NR4 information return; see Guide T4061 NR4 – Non-Resident Tax Withholding, Remitting, and Reporting for details.

Note for Represent a Client (RAC) users

As a user of the Represent a Client service, you can only open a non-resident tax account on behalf of a Canadian payer or withholding agent.

Based on the level of authorization your client granted you under their primary CRA identifier, the account number and address may not be visible to you. Correspondence will be sent to the account owner to confirm their account number.

Authorization levels:

Level 1 – The CRA can disclose information to you about your client's account.

Level 2 – The CRA can disclose information to you and make changes to your client's account.

Level 3 – Legal representative. An individual or organization legally authorized to act on behalf of the taxpayer as though they were the taxpayer themselves. The limit of the actual authority is defined by the legal document, such as a power of attorney.

Your client has to send us Form AUT-01 Authorize a Representative for Access by Phone and Mail, if they want you to be authorized on their non-resident tax account or if they want to change the authorization level they gave you.

What is the role of the account owner?

There are three types of account owners:

Canadian payer: a Canadian resident who pays or credits Part XIII amounts to a non-resident of Canada

Withholding agent: a person who receives Part XIII amounts, from which tax was not withheld, on behalf of a non-resident of Canada

Non-resident payee: a non-resident of Canada who is receiving Part XIII amounts from a Canadian payer

Income source

Choose the source of income earned in Canada that is paid or credited to the non-resident.

If the Canadian-source income is from rent or timber royalties, select the first option. You will then be prompted to select one of two choices: gross rents from real property, or timber royalties.

If the income is from other sources, such as dividends, trust, pensions or other income subject to Part XIII withholding tax, select the second option.

A separate non-resident tax account will have to be created if the non-resident is being paid or credited rental income and income from other sources, such as dividend, trust, pension or other income subject to Part XIII withholding tax.

Add/Modify rental property - information

This screen provides information about the non-resident rental property owner (if the account is being created by a withholding agent or Canadian payer) and the rental property. 

Non-resident name: enter the non-resident's given name first, then surname; e.g., John Smith

Non-resident type: select the type of non-resident property owner from the dropdowns

Non-resident CRA account number (e.g., SIN/ITN, BN, or T3 number): enter the non-resident's CRA account number, if you know it

Note: The CRA will retain only the information that matches its records.

Rental start date: enter the date the property began to generate income. If unknown, enter today's date.

Rental payment frequency: select from the dropdowns provided how often the rental income will be paid or credited to the non-resident:

monthly: income is paid once a month

quarterly: income is paid every three months

annually: income is paid once a year

irregular: income is paid at irregular intervals and not always at the same time every year

one-time payment: income is not continuous and is paid all at once

other: includes payments made weekly, bi-weekly, bi-annually, every two months, or at other intervals

Gross annual rental income: Enter the gross annual rental income for the calendar year, in Canadian dollars.

Percentage of ownership (1 to 100): If known, enter the non-resident's percentage of ownership. Round to the nearest whole number; for example, round 33.33% to 33% and round 16.66% to 17%.

Payment frequency

The payment frequency is how often the income is paid or credited to the non-resident. The payment frequency often depends on the type of income. For example, rental property income is most commonly paid monthly and dividend income is usually paid quarterly.

Payment date: when the income is paid or credited to the non-resident.

Remittance date: Part XIII withholding tax remittance is due by the 15th of the month following the month in which the non-resident was paid or credited the Canadian-source income. For example, if the income was paid to a non-resident any day in the month of April, the Part XIII tax is due no later than May 15.

Payment start date

From the dropdown menu, select the month the non-resident will begin being paid or credited income from Canada. If you don't know, select “unknown.”

Payment frequency    

Select from the dropdown provided how often the non-resident will be paid or credited Canadian-source income:

monthly: income is paid once a month

quarterly: income is paid every three months (i.e., four times a year)

annually: income is paid once a year

irregular: income is paid at irregular intervals and at varied times throughout a calendar year

one-time payment: income is paid all at once

other: includes payments made weekly, bi-weekly, bi-annually, every two months, or at other intervals

unknown: The payment frequency is unknown

Payment frequency

The payment start date shows the month and year the non-resident will begin to be paid or credited the Canadian-source income.

Part XIII withholding tax is due by the 15th day of the month following the month in which the Canadian-source income was paid or credited to the non-resident. For example, if the non-resident received the income on any day in April, the withholding tax remittance is due by May 15. 

The payment frequency is how often the payments will be paid or credited to the non-resident. It can be monthly, quarterly, annually, one-time payment, etc.
